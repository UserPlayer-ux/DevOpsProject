F1 Driver

1. Lewis Hamilton: A seven-time World Champion with 105 Grand Prix victories,
Hamilton has recently joined Ferrari, partnering with Charles Leclerc for the 2025 season.

2. Michael Schumacher: Also a seven-time World Champion,
Schumacher secured 91 Grand Prix wins during his illustrious career.

3. Max Verstappen: The Dutch driver has clinched four consecutive World Championships from 2021 to 2024,
amassing 63 Grand Prix victories.

4. Sebastian Vettel: A four-time World Champion with 53 Grand Prix wins,
Vettel retired from Formula 1 at the end of the 2022 season.

5. Alain Prost: The French driver secured four World Championships and achieved 51 Grand Prix victories during his career.

6. Ayrton Senna: A three-time World Champion,
Senna is celebrated for his exceptional skill and 41 Grand Prix wins before his untimely death in 1994.

7. Fernando Alonso: The Spanish driver has two World Championships and 32 Grand Prix victories to his name,
continuing to compete actively in Formula 1.

8. Niki Lauda: A three-time World Champion,
Lauda achieved 25 Grand Prix wins and is renowned for his remarkable comeback following a near-fatal accident in 1976.

9. Jim Clark: The Scottish driver won two World Championships and secured 25 Grand Prix victories before his career was tragically cut short in 1968.

10. Juan Manuel Fangio: An Argentine driver who dominated the early years of Formula 1,
winning five World Championships in the 1950s.

11. Kimi Raikkonen: nicknamed "The Iceman", won the 2007 Formula One World Championship while driving for Ferrari; he finished
second overall twice and third three times.
